PHOTOSHOOT EFFECT

okeh refers to the aesthetic quality
of blur, the out of focus area
of the image. It’s how the light renders
lighted areas that are out of focus.
The difference in lens aberrations and aperture
shape causes the area to blur, creating
the look that’s aesthetically pleasing.
Many photographers deliberately use
the shallow-focus technique to create
images with prominent bokeh areas. The term comes from
Japanese ‘boke’, which literally means haze or blur.
